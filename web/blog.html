How Plantify works

Short, technical notes on the model, the decision funnel, and how to get a good result from the Identify page.

Model

How Plantify Identifies a Leaf

Upload a photo and Plantify runs it through a MobileNetV2-based classifier trained on 15 leaf species. Before trusting the result, two checks run: a quality check that the photo looks like a clean leaf scan, and an out-of-distribution check that compares the photo's learned features against the training data. Depending on how confident and how in-domain the photo is, you'll see one of three outcomes — a confident match (ok), a possible match worth double-checking (uncertain), or an honest "couldn't identify this" (unknown) rather than a guess. Try it on the Identify page.

Photo tips

Getting a Good Photo for Identification

The model was trained on clean scans of single leaves against a plain, near-white background, so it does best with similar photos. A few tips: lay the leaf flat on a plain sheet of paper or a light table, fill most of the frame with just the one leaf, avoid harsh shadows or background clutter, and make sure it's in focus. Busy backgrounds or photos with multiple leaves are more likely to come back uncertain or unknown — that's the quality guard doing its job instead of guessing.

Automation

How Plantify Retrains Itself

When a photo comes back unknown, the API can optionally ask Pl@ntNet for a second opinion. A confident answer is staged — image and label — to a dedicated contributions branch, never main directly. Every Monday, a GitHub Actions job evaluates the currently committed model, promotes qualifying candidates into the training set, retrains, and re-evaluates. The update ships only if a regression gate confirms both overall accuracy and every individual species' recall held up — otherwise it's discarded and an issue records why. Accepted retrains open a pull request and auto-merge, committed honestly as github-actions[bot], never disguised as manual work. Full design in the repo's architecture docs.

Species guide

The 15 Species Plantify Knows

Plantify's model is trained on the Swedish Leaf Dataset's 15 species: Acer, Alnus incana, Betula pubescens, Fagus silvatica, Populus, Populus tremula, Quercus, Salix alba 'Sericea', Salix aurita, Salix cinerea, Sorbus aucuparia, Sorbus intermedia, Tilia, Ulmus carpinifolia, and Ulmus glabra. A photo of anything outside this list — or a low-quality photo of one of these species — should come back as uncertain or unknown rather than a confident wrong answer.
